Manual / Overview

00 Overview

The SMS Enterprises ERP runs on ODOO 19, extended with a custom addon suite covering attendance, leave, payroll, projects, expenses, and documents.

What the system covers

  • Attendance — check-in/check-out, late detection, half-day leave logic, company holidays.
  • Leave — Casual, Earned, Medical, and Permission leave, with anniversary-based allocation and carry-forward.
  • Payroll — monthly salary computation, driven by your attendance and leave register.
  • Projects — team-based tasks, kanban boards, cross-team assignment approval.
  • Expenses — submission, approval, and Excel export for records.
  • Documents — employee document upload with inline preview.
How access works here The sections you see are read directly from your employee role in the ERP — the same role that already controls your permissions elsewhere in the system. There's nothing to select; it's not something this page can get wrong or something you can override from the browser.
Manual / Your Desk

01 Attendance & Check-In

One check-in, one check-out, once a day. The system tracks lateness automatically and talks to your leave balance behind the scenes.

How to check in or out

  1. Go to Attendances → Check In / Check Out (or use the button on your Home dashboard).
  2. A popup shows your current status. If you're not checked in, click Check In.
  3. To check out later, open the same menu again — the button now says Check Out. Click it.

You can only complete one full check-in/check-out cycle per day. Once you've checked out, the button won't let you check in again until the next day.

Late threshold — 9:30 AM Checking in after 9:30 AM marks the day as Late and starts drawing from your monthly Permission buffer (see Section 03). On a day you have an approved AM half-day leave, the threshold shifts to the company's afternoon session start time instead (currently 2:00 PM) — you're not "late" until after that.

What happens if you check in during your own AM leave

If you have an approved AM half-day leave for today and you still check in during the morning, the system doesn't do anything automatically — your leave stays approved, and no late minutes are calculated for that check-in, since the late threshold is still shifted to the afternoon start time.

If this needs correcting (for example, you actually worked the full day and shouldn't have taken the AM leave), HR must manually refuse the AM leave record from the Time Off screen. Once HR refuses it, the system recalculates your lateness against the normal 9:30 AM threshold for that day.

Employees If your plans changed and you ended up working the morning after all, let HR know so they can refuse the leave on your behalf — it won't happen by itself.

If you forget to check out

The system auto-closes any open session at 7:00 PM your local time. This shows on your record as Auto Checkout. HR and Managers get notified automatically whenever this happens to you.

Holidays and Saturdays

  • On a declared company holiday, check-in is blocked with a message telling you why.
  • The 2nd and 4th Saturday of every month are automatically treated as days off — no check-in needed, nothing to configure.
  • If HR has marked a specific Saturday as a Compensatory Working Day (see Section 13), check-in works normally that day even though it's a Saturday.

Viewing your own records

Go to Attendances → My Attendance → My Records to see your check-in/check-out history, hours worked, and which days were flagged late or auto-closed.

02 Leave & Half-Days

Three leave types, an anniversary-based year, and half-days split cleanly into AM and PM.

Leave types & entitlement

Type Annual Quota Carry-Forward
Casual Leave (CL) 12 days Up to 50% (max 6 days)
Earned Leave (EL) 3 days Full carry-forward
Medical Leave (ML) 3 days Full carry-forward

Your leave year runs from your joining anniversary, not the calendar year. Each year on that date, the system automatically closes your previous cycle and opens a new one — unused leave carries forward per the table above, and your fresh quota is added on top.

Days only — no hourly requests Casual, Earned, and Medical Leave are all requested in full or half days. If you need to request time off in hours instead, that's a different leave type — see Section 03, Permission Buffer.

How to request leave

  1. Go to Time Off → New.
  2. Choose the Time Off Type — Casual Leave, Earned Leave, or Medical Leave. The dropdown shows your remaining balance for each (e.g. "Earned Leave (3 remaining out of 3 days)").
  3. Set the Date range. For a single day, set the start and end date to the same day.
  4. Under Day Period, you'll see two fields: Morning and Afternoon.
    • To take the full day off, leave both as they are (the system defaults to covering the whole day).
    • To take a half day, select only Morning or only Afternoon — picking just one half is what makes it a half-day request instead of a full day.
  5. Optionally attach a file (e.g. a medical certificate) using Attach File.
  6. Click Submit Request. It goes to HR for approval — you'll get a notification once it's approved or refused.
No backdating You can't submit a leave request for a date that has already passed. If you need a past date recorded (e.g. you were out sick and didn't file it in time), ask HR to enter it on your behalf — only HR can backdate a leave request.
All-holiday requests are blocked If every single day in your requested range is already a company holiday, the system won't let you submit the request at all — there's nothing to take leave from. Pick working days only.

Half-day mechanics

A half-day leave (Morning only, or Afternoon only) is recorded as its own separate entry — it is not a fraction of a full-day record. This means:

  • You can mix leave types on the same day — for example, submit Casual Leave for the Morning and Earned Leave for the Afternoon as two separate requests.
  • Each half can be approved or refused independently by HR — approving your Morning half doesn't automatically approve the Afternoon half, and vice versa.
Checking in during an approved Morning leave If you have an approved Morning half-day leave but check in that morning anyway, nothing happens automatically — your leave stays approved and you won't be marked late (see Section 01). If your leave should be corrected, ask HR to manually refuse it.

Checking your balance

Go to Time Off → My Time Off to see your current balance for each leave type, and your request history.

Allocation requests (asking for more than your quota)

If you need more days than your current allocation allows, you can submit an allocation request from Time Off → My Allocations → New. This does not get approved automatically — every allocation request, for every leave type, requires HR sign-off before the extra days are added to your balance.

03 Permission Buffer

A monthly 4-hour cushion for late arrivals — used automatically, or claimed proactively. Unlike Casual/Earned/Medical Leave, Permission is requested in hours, not days.

How it works

Every employee gets a flat 4 hours (240 minutes) of Permission leave on the 1st of every month. It does not carry forward, and there's no proration — everyone gets the full 240 minutes regardless of when in the month they joined or how much they used last month.

→

→
You're notified

Requesting Permission time proactively

You can draw on the same pool ahead of time — for a dentist appointment, say — instead of waiting for a late check-in to use it up.

  1. Go to Time Off → New.
  2. Set Time Off Type to Permission.
  3. Enter the date and the number of hours you need.
  4. Submit — like other leave types, this goes to HR for approval.
Manual requests are used first If you've already submitted and been approved for Permission hours on a given day, those hours are subtracted from your late-arrival minutes before the automatic deduction kicks in. In other words, a manual request that morning reduces (or fully covers) any lateness deduction for that same day, instead of stacking on top of it.

What happens when you're late

If you check in after 9:30 AM (or after the shifted threshold on a half-day-leave day — see Section 01), the system automatically:

  1. Calculates your late minutes.
  2. Subtracts any manual Permission hours you already have approved for that day.
  3. Deducts the remaining late minutes from your monthly Permission balance.
  4. Sends you a notification confirming how many minutes were deducted and what your remaining balance is.

You'll get a notification every time a late-arrival deduction happens — not just when your balance runs low.

This doesn't double-count If your manual Permission request already covers your actual arrival time, no additional minutes are deducted — the system subtracts your approved manual hours from your late minutes first, and only deducts anything extra if you arrive later than what you'd requested.
If the pool runs out Once your 4-hour buffer is exhausted for the month, further late minutes are not deducted from Permission — instead, they're recorded as overflow and deducted directly from your salary in that month's payroll (see Section 11).

On top of the per-deduction notification, you'll get an extra warning when your remaining balance drops to 30 minutes or below, and again when it hits zero.

Checking your balance

Go to Time Off → My Time Off and look for Permission in your list of leave types to see your remaining minutes for the current month.

04 Expenses

Submit, attach a receipt, and track approval status.

How to submit an expense

  1. Go to Expenses → My Expenses and click New.
  2. Fill in:
    • Category — e.g. Travel, Stationery, Office Snacks, Utilities, Software/Subscriptions, or Miscellaneous.
    • Amount.
    • Related Project — optional, only if the expense should be tied to a specific project.
    • Expense Date.
    • Description — required, explain what the expense was for.
  3. Under Attachments, attach your bill or invoice proof.
  4. Click Submit for Approval.
Draft vs Submitted While your expense is in Draft, you can still edit every field. Once you click Submit for Approval, it moves to Submitted and becomes read-only until a decision is made.

What happens after you submit

Your submission notifies HR and Managers. One of them will either:

  • Approve it — you get notified that it's approved. Reimbursement is handled separately by HR outside the system.
  • Reject it — you get notified with a written reason. The expense moves to Rejected status, showing that reason on the form.

If your expense is rejected

  1. Open the rejected expense from Expenses → My Expenses.
  2. Read the rejection reason shown on the form.
  3. Click Reset to Draft — this is available only on rejected expenses.
  4. Make your corrections and submit again.

Tracking your expenses

The My Expenses list shows all your submissions grouped by status (Draft, Submitted, Approved, Rejected), with the amount, category, and attachment count visible at a glance.

Excel export

From the Expenses list view, use the export option to download a summary of your own expense history as an Excel file for your own records.

05 Work Reports

A short daily record of what you worked on — submitted to your Manager, no approval step, just a submit/reset workflow.

How to file a report

  1. Go to Work Reports → My Reports and click New.
  2. Fill in:
    • Date — defaults to today.
    • Project — optional, link it if the work relates to a specific project.
    • Hours Spent.
    • Work Done — a description of what you accomplished.
  3. Under Attachments, attach any relevant files — they open with a Preview button that shows the file in a new tab, so there's no need to download to view them.
  4. Click Submit.
There's no approval step Unlike Leave or Expenses, a Work Report doesn't require anyone to approve or reject it. Submitting simply notifies everyone in the Work Report Manager group that your report is in — it's a record for visibility, not a request awaiting a decision.

Draft vs Submitted

While a report is in Draft, you can freely edit it. Once you click Submit, it moves to Submitted and becomes read-only for you.

Only a Manager can reset a submitted report to Draft If you need to correct a report after submitting it, you can't do this yourself — ask your Manager to click Reset to Draft on the record. Once it's back in Draft, you can edit and resubmit it.

Viewing your reports

Go to Work Reports → My Reports to see your full history, grouped by status, with the date, project, hours, and state visible in the list.

For Managers & HR — reviewing team reports

Go to Work Reports → All Reports to see every submitted report across the team. You'll also get an inbox notification each time an employee submits one. If an employee needs to correct a submitted report, open it and click Reset to Draft — this unlocks it for them to edit and resubmit.

Your payslip and how HR keeps your personal documents on file.

Your documents

Employee document uploads are handled by HR You don't upload or manage your own ID proofs, certificates, or contracts directly. If you need to submit a document (ID proof, certificate, offer letter, etc.), hand it to HR — they'll upload it to your employee record. You won't see a Documents menu of your own for this.

Viewing your payslip

  1. Go to Employees → My Payslips.
  2. You'll see a list of your payslips grouped by status.
  3. Open any payslip to see the breakdown: earnings, deductions (including any Permission Overflow or Absent Day deductions), and net pay.
  4. Use the Print Payslip button on the payslip form to generate a PDF copy.

Your payslip reflects your attendance, leave, and any Permission overflow deductions for that month once HR has run and confirmed the monthly payroll (see Section 11).

07 Projects, Teams & Tasks

How teams work, how to create and progress a task, and how timesheets and cross-team assignment actually behave.

What a "Team" is

A Team groups employees together under a Team Lead. Every project is tied to a team, and most task visibility is scoped by team membership.

  • You can see a project or task if: it belongs to your team, it has no team assigned at all, you created it, you're assigned to it, or you assigned it to someone else.
  • You cannot see tasks or projects belonging to other teams, unless you're a Team Lead, Manager, or HR.

To see which team(s) you belong to, open your own profile — HR/Manager profiles show a Teams smart button; you can also ask HR if you're unsure.

How to create a task

  1. Go to Tasks → My Tasks (or open a project and add a task from its kanban board).
  2. Click New — this always opens the full task form, not a quick-create card.
  3. Fill in the task name, select the Project (this auto-fills the Team if the project has one), and set Assigned To.
  4. Save.
Only the creator can fully edit a task If you didn't create a task, you can't edit most of its fields — even if it's assigned to you. You'll see a read-only banner at the top of the form. What you can still do on a task assigned to you: change its state (see below) and log timesheet entries. Managers and HR can edit any task regardless of who created it.

Task states — how to progress a task

Every task moves through a fixed sequence: Draft → Assigned → In Progress → Completed → Verified → Closed. Only the employee(s) a task is assigned to can move it forward (Managers, Team Leads, and HR can do this for any task):

  • Start — moves Assigned → In Progress.
  • Mark Complete — moves In Progress → Completed.
  • Verify — moves Completed → Verified.
  • Close — moves Verified → Closed (this is the final state).

These buttons appear on the task form's header, and only the button relevant to the task's current state is shown.

Only a Manager can reset a task to Draft If a closed or in-progress task needs to be reopened from scratch, that's a Manager-only action — ask your Manager.

Assigning a task to someone — and what happens across teams

When you add someone to a task's Assigned To field:

  • If that person is on the same team as the task, they're assigned immediately — no approval needed.
  • If that person is on a different team, the system doesn't assign them right away. Instead, it creates a cross-team assignment request and sends it to Managers and HR for approval. The task stays visible to that person as "pending" until the request is decided.
  • Managers can assign across teams freely — the approval step doesn't apply to them.

Tracking your assignment requests

Go to Assignment Requests → My Requests to see anything you've requested and its status (Pending, Approved, Rejected). You'll be notified either way once a Manager or HR makes a decision.

Logging timesheets

  1. Open the task you worked on.
  2. Go to the Timesheets tab and add a line: date, hours, a short description, and — if relevant — the Software Used field, a free-text note on what tool/application you used.
  3. Save.
You can see everyone's timesheet entries, but only edit your own Every employee can view all timesheet entries across the whole company (useful for coordinating on shared tasks), but you can only create, edit, or delete your own entries. Rows belonging to someone else appear read-only. HR and Managers can edit anyone's entries.

Task stages (kanban columns)

Task stages (e.g. Requirement, Development, Testing, Done) are a shared library managed by HR/Managers — you can't create a brand-new stage on the fly from a project's kanban board. If you type a stage name that already exists in the library while adding a column, it links that existing stage to your project. If it doesn't exist yet, ask a Manager or HR to add it under Configuration → Task Stages.

Team Lead — what's different

If you're set as the Team Lead of a team, you get some extra visibility and rights your teammates don't have:

  • You can submit a Project Completion Request for your team's project (see below) — regular team members cannot.
  • You still cannot approve cross-team assignment requests — that stays Manager/HR only.

Requesting project completion

When a project is fully done, it needs to be formally marked complete and locked. This can only be requested by the project's Team Lead, an HR officer, or a Manager — not by regular team members.

  1. Open the project and click Request Completion.
  2. Confirm the prompt. This creates a completion request and notifies Managers.
  3. Track it under Completion Requests → My Completion Requests.
Only a Manager approves — not HR Unlike most approvals in this system, project completion is decided by Managers only. Once approved, the project moves to its Done stage and is locked — after that, only a Manager can edit the project or its tasks, until a Manager clicks Reopen.

08 Manufacturing

Manufacturing Orders (MOs) run on standard Odoo Manufacturing, extended with Assembly/Production typing, component tracking, cost analysis, and serial number + warranty generation.

Who sees this menu The Manufacturing menu only appears for employees in a Manufacturing-named department, or anyone HR/Manager has explicitly given manufacturing access via Setup Permissions (see Section 14). If you don't see it and believe you should, ask HR.

Assembly vs Production — what the type means

Every Bill of Materials (BOM) is tagged as Assembly, Production, or Assembly + Production. A Manufacturing Order (MO) automatically inherits this type from the BOM it's built on — you don't set it directly on the MO.

  • Assembly orders show an "Assembly Details" tab (start/end dates, client requirements, step-by-step assembly instructions).
  • Production orders show a "Production Details" tab (start/end dates, process notes) instead.
  • Assembly + Production orders show both tabs.

Standard Odoo manufacturing flow

The core MO lifecycle is unmodified Odoo Manufacturing:

Draft
→
Confirmed
→
In Progress
→
Done
  1. Go to Manufacturing → Assembly Orders or Production Orders (these are the same underlying MO list, pre-filtered by manufacturing type) and click New.
  2. Select the Product — its BOM's manufacturing type and warranty period carry over automatically.
  3. Set the Client and, if useful, Client Requirements.
  4. Click Confirm to reserve components, then Mark as Done once production finishes — standard Odoo behavior throughout.

Bills of Materials — components and operations

A BOM defines what it takes to build one unit of a product. Go to Manufacturing → Products → Bills of Materials, open or create one, and fill in two tabs:

  • Components tab — click Add a line for each raw material, sub-assembly, bought-out part, or consumable and set its quantity. This module adds a Component Type field (Raw Material / Sub Assembly / Bought Out Part / Consumable), an is_critical checkbox, and a free-text notes column on top of the standard component line.
  • Operations tab — click Add a line to define each production step (e.g. "Cut", "Assemble", "Paint"). Each operation requires a Work Center and a Default Duration — this is what drives both scheduling and cost.

Work Centers — where operations actually happen

A Work Center is a machine, workstation, or production line — e.g. "CNC Machine 01" or "Assembly Line A". Go to Manufacturing → Configuration → Work Centers to set one up:

  • Capacity — how many units it can process in parallel.
  • Time Efficiency — a percentage used to adjust planned durations against real-world output (default 100%).
  • Working Hours — the resource calendar the work center follows, used for scheduling.
  • Equipment tab — optionally link specific machines/tools to the work center, each with a technician, MTBF (mean time between failures), and MTTR (mean time to recovery) for basic maintenance tracking.

Work Orders must be enabled first: Manufacturing → Configuration → Settings → Work Orders. Once enabled, every operation on a BOM requires a work center, and every MO built from that BOM gets a Work Orders tab showing each step in sequence.

Processing Work Orders on an MO

Once an MO is confirmed, open its Work Orders tab to see each operation from the BOM, in order, with its assigned work center and planned duration. For each work order:

  1. Click the work order line to open it.
  2. Click Start — this logs the actual start time and moves the work order to "In Progress".
  3. Once the step is physically done, click Mark as Done.
  4. Repeat for each subsequent operation. The MO as a whole is only ready to close once all its work orders are done.
No Shop Floor tablet app on Community Odoo Enterprise has a dedicated Shop Floor module — a full-screen tablet interface for operators to tap through work orders on the production floor. That module is not available on Community. On this installation, work orders are processed from the regular MO form's Work Orders tab as described above.

How operations feed into MO cost

Beyond the component cost already covered under Cost Analysis, standard Odoo also factors in operation time and work center cost when a work center has an hourly cost configured — visible on the base Odoo cost breakdown, separate from this module's Labour Cost / Overhead Cost fields, which are for costs you want to enter manually per order.

1-step vs 2-step vs 3-step manufacturing routes

How components and finished goods physically move between stock, production, and back to stock is controlled per product/warehouse under Inventory → Configuration → Warehouses, or per-product route settings:

Route What happens
1-step (Manufacture) Components are consumed straight from stock; the MO alone handles the movement.
2-step (Pick + Manufacture) A separate picking operation stages components at a production location first, then the MO consumes from there.
3-step (Pick + Manufacture + Store) Adds a third step that puts the finished product away to its final storage location after production.

This is a warehouse-level configuration decision, not something you set per MO — ask HR/Manager if you're unsure which route your warehouse uses.

Community edition limits worth knowing A few manufacturing-adjacent features you may see referenced in Odoo's official documentation or online guides are Enterprise-only and not present here: the Shop Floor tablet app, the Quality app (statistical process control, quality checkpoints), PLM (engineering change orders), advanced MRP-II scheduling, and IoT device integration. Serial number tracking, warranty, and job-card printing on this system are handled by this custom module instead.

Checking component availability

The Components stat button on the MO form (top right) shows "available / total" at a glance. Click Check Components in the header for a full breakdown — each component is listed as either sufficient (✅) or short (❌) against what's required.

Component Status Meaning
Components Pending None of the required components are fully available yet
Partially Available Some, but not all, components are available
All Components Ready Every component has enough quantity on hand

Cost analysis

Open the Cost Analysis tab on any MO to see:

  • Component Cost — computed automatically from each component's standard cost × quantity required.
  • Labour Cost and Overhead Cost — enter these manually.
  • Total Cost, Selling Price, Profit, and Profit % — calculated live as you fill in the fields above, with a Profit / Loss / Break Even badge.

Serial numbers & warranties

Once an order is producing real, trackable units, click Generate Serial Numbers (the Serials stat button, or from the header) to open the serial wizard:

  1. Set the Quantity of serials to generate, and confirm the Client.
  2. Set Warranty Years (defaults from the product's BOM) and Warranty Start date.
  3. Click Generate Preview to see the serial numbers that will be created.
  4. Click Confirm & Create — this creates one Serial Number record and one linked Warranty record per unit, all in one step.

Warranties automatically show as Active, Expiring Soon (30 days or less remaining), or Expired, and can be browsed under Manufacturing → Warranties (All / Active / Expiring Soon). A daily background check logs any warranties entering the expiring or expired state.

Printing a warranty certificate Open any Warranty record and click Print Certificate to generate a client-ready PDF with product, serial number, client, and warranty period details.

Printing the Job Card

Click Print Job Card in the MO header (available once the order is out of Draft) for a PDF summary of the order: components with required vs. available quantities and critical-part flags, assembly or production instructions, the full cost breakdown, and any serial numbers generated so far — handy as a shop-floor traveler document.

09 Approving Leave

Every leave and allocation request — Casual, Earned, Medical, and Permission — routes to HR for a decision.

How to approve or refuse a leave request

  1. Go to Time Off → Management → Time Off.
  2. Open the request you want to review.
  3. Check the dates, leave type, and any attached document (e.g. a medical certificate).
  4. Click Approve or Refuse.

The employee is notified automatically either way — you don't need to message them separately.

Half-days approve independently A Morning half-day and an Afternoon half-day on the same date are two separate records. Approving one does not approve the other — check and decide each one on its own.

What to check before approving

  • Whether the request overlaps a company holiday — the system already blocks requests where every requested day is a holiday, but partial overlaps still go through, so check the actual working days involved.
  • Whether a half-day request makes sense against that day's workload.
  • Medical Leave requests should ideally have a certificate attached for anything beyond a single day.

Approving allocation requests

If an employee requests more days than their current balance allows (via Time Off → My Allocations on their end), it shows up for you the same way:

  1. Go to Time Off → Management → Allocations.
  2. Open the pending allocation request.
  3. Click Approve or Refuse.
Every allocation needs HR approval — including your own All three leave types (Casual, Earned, Medical) are configured so that no allocation is added automatically, even for the employee who requested it. Every single allocation request must be explicitly approved by HR before those days count toward the employee's balance.

Correcting a leave after the fact

If an employee has an approved Morning half-day leave but checked in that day anyway, the system doesn't auto-correct anything (see Section 01). You need to manually refuse the leave from Time Off → Approvals — this automatically triggers a lateness recalculation for that day. See Section 12 for the full walkthrough.

10 Approving Expenses

Review team expense claims before they're settled outside the system.

How to approve an expense

  1. Go to Expenses → All Expenses.
  2. Open a submitted expense.
  3. Check the amount, category, description, and attached receipt/invoice.
  4. Click Approve.

The employee is notified automatically that their expense was approved.

How to reject an expense

  1. Open the submitted expense.
  2. Click Reject — this opens a small wizard.
  3. Enter a Rejection Reason. This field is mandatory; you can't reject without one.
  4. Click Confirm Rejection.

The employee is notified with your reason, and the expense moves to Rejected status. They can then use Reset to Draft on their end to correct and resubmit it — you don't need to do anything further at that point.

Filtering the queue

Expenses → All Expenses defaults to showing Submitted (pending) expenses first, so you can work through the approval queue without extra filtering. Use the search filters (Draft / Submitted / Approved / Rejected) to look at other states.

Managing expense categories

Go to Expenses → Configuration → Expense Categories to add, rename, or archive categories (Travel, Stationery, Office Snacks, Utilities, Software/Subscriptions, Miscellaneous, etc.). This is HR Manager only.

11 Projects & Task Assignment

Cross-team assignment decisions, task stage configuration, and locking a project as complete. For how employees create tasks, log timesheets, and progress task states, see Section 06B.

Approving cross-team assignment requests

When someone assigns a task to an employee on a different team, the system holds it as a pending request instead of assigning immediately (unless the person assigning is a Manager or HR, who can assign across teams freely).

  1. Go to Assignment Requests → All Requests.
  2. Open a pending request — it shows the task, who requested it, the requesting team, and the target employee/team.
  3. Click Approve or Reject.

Approving assigns the employee to the task immediately and notifies them. Rejecting leaves the task unassigned and notifies the requester with any comment you leave.

Both HR and Managers can decide these Unlike project completion (below), cross-team assignment requests can be approved or rejected by either an HR officer or a Manager — there's no restriction here.

Approving project completion requests

A completion request can only be submitted by the project's Team Lead, an HR officer, or a Manager — but it can only be approved by a Manager.

  1. Go to Completion Requests → All Completion Requests.
  2. Open a pending request.
  3. Click Approve or Reject.
HR cannot approve project completion — Managers only This is one of the few approvals in the system where HR is deliberately excluded. If you're an HR officer, you can submit a completion request on behalf of a team, but a Manager has to make the final call.

Approving a request moves the project to its Done stage and locks it — from that point on, only a Manager can edit the project or its tasks. To unlock it later, open the project and click Reopen Project (Manager only) — this restores it to whatever stage it was on before the lock.

Managing task stages

Task stages are a shared library, not per-project — the same set of stages (e.g. Requirement, Analysis & Planning, Development, Internal Testing, Client Review, Implementation, Maintenance/Support, Done) is available across every project.

  1. Go to Configuration → Task Stages.
  2. Click New to add a stage, or edit an existing one inline.
  3. Toggle Auto-add to New Projects if the stage should automatically appear on every new project's kanban board.
  4. Toggle Folded by Default if the stage's column should start collapsed in kanban view (typically used for "Done").
Employees can't create new stages on the fly If an employee tries to add a new kanban column with a name that doesn't already exist in the stage library, the system blocks it. They can only pick from stages you've already created here.

Full project and task visibility

As HR or Manager, you see every project and task regardless of team — the team-based visibility restriction that applies to regular employees doesn't apply to you.

12 Leave Policy & Allocations

How the anniversary-based leave year, quotas, and carry-forward are administered — and where the automation ends and manual work begins.

Policy at a glance

Type Quota Carry-Forward Cap
Casual Leave 12 days / year 6 days (50%)
Earned Leave 3 days / year 3 days (full)
Medical Leave 3 days / year 3 days (full)
All three types require HR approval to allocate Casual, Earned, and Medical Leave are all configured with allocation approval required — no employee (or HR user, for that matter) can self-approve an allocation. Every allocation, whether generated automatically or requested manually, must go through an HR approval step before it counts toward the employee's balance.

Initial allocation — new employees

When a new employee record is created, the system automatically generates their first cycle's allocation for all three leave types, based on their joining date (read from their contract start date, or their record creation date as a fallback). This happens without any manual action — you don't need to create these allocations yourself.

Anniversary carry-forward

A scheduled job runs daily and checks every active employee. If today matches an employee's joining-date anniversary (month and day), the system:

  1. Closes their previous leave-year allocation.
  2. Calculates unused days from the closing cycle, capped at each type's carry-forward limit (6 for Casual, full 3 for Earned/Medical).
  3. Opens a new allocation for the new cycle: fresh quota + carried-forward days.

This is fully automatic — you don't need to trigger it manually, and it's safe even if it somehow ran twice for the same employee on the same day (it checks for an existing allocation for that start date first).

Manual allocation edits

If you need to adjust an employee's allocation directly (a correction, a special grant, etc.):

  1. Go to Time Off → Allocations.
  2. Open or create the allocation record for the employee and leave type.
  3. Edit the Number of Days.
  4. Approve the record — it won't count toward their balance until it's in the Approved state.
After changing an employee's System Role Changing an employee's System Role (Employee / HR / Manager) on their profile does not automatically update their system permissions. You must open their profile and click Setup Permissions for the new role's access to actually take effect.

Permission leave allocation

A separate monthly job runs on the 1st of every month and grants every active employee a flat 4-hour (240-minute) Permission allocation for that month. There's no carry-forward and no proration — this runs automatically and needs no manual step from HR, unless an employee's Permission allocation is somehow missing (in which case, create it manually the same way as above, using the Permission leave type).

13 Payroll Processing

Monthly payroll draws directly from the attendance and leave register — the order you run things in matters.

Finalize leave records
→
Generate & Confirm Monthly Summary
→
Create payslip
→
Compute & validate
Order matters — the summary must be Confirmed, not just generated A payslip only picks up attendance/leave data from a Monthly Summary that is in the Confirmed state. If the summary for that employee and month is still in Draft, or doesn't exist yet, the payslip falls back to a default 30-day denominator and zero absent/overflow deduction — it will not wait for you to confirm it later; you'd need to recompute the payslip after confirming the summary.

Step 1 — Generate the Monthly Summary

  1. Go to Attendance → Generate Summaries (Batch).
  2. Select the Month and Year (defaults to last month).
  3. Optionally pick specific employees — leave blank to generate for everyone active.
  4. Click Generate Summaries.

This creates or recomputes each employee's working days, present days, late days, leave days, and absent days for that month.

Step 2 — Confirm the summary

  1. Go to Attendance → Monthly Summaries.
  2. Open each employee's summary, review the numbers.
  3. Click Confirm and Lock.

Once confirmed, a summary can't be edited unless you explicitly reset it to Draft first — this is what protects it from being accidentally recalculated after payroll has already used it.

Step 3 — Create and compute the payslip

  1. Go to Payroll → Employee Payslips, and create a new payslip (or use Payslip Batches ).
  2. Set the Month and Year directly on the payslip — this automatically sets the underlying pay period dates.
  3. Click Compute Sheet. This is the point where the system pulls in that employee's confirmed Monthly Summary and injects the absent-day and overflow figures as payslip inputs.
  4. Review the computed lines, then confirm/validate the payslip as your payroll process requires.

What feeds the payslip, and the exact math

  • Unpaid Absent Days — pulled from the confirmed Monthly Summary (absences + unpaid leave combined).
  • Working Days — also from the confirmed summary; falls back to 30 if no confirmed summary exists for that period.
  • Absent Deduction — calculated as (wage ÷ working_days) × absent_days.
  • Permission Overflow Deduction — late minutes beyond the monthly 4-hour Permission buffer, calculated as ((wage ÷ working_days) ÷ 540) × overflow_minutes (540 minutes = a 9-hour working day).

Common issues

Symptom Likely cause Fix
Payslip shows unexpectedly low or zero deductions Monthly Summary for that period isn't Confirmed yet Confirm the summary, then recompute the payslip
Working days shows as 30 for everyone No confirmed summary exists for the payslip's period Generate and confirm the summary first, then recompute
No Permission Overflow line on the payslip Employee had no overflow minutes that month, or the summary wasn't confirmed Check the summary's Permission Overflow Minutes field

Printing a payslip

Open the payslip and click Print Payslip in the header — this generates the official SMS Enterprises-branded PDF layout.

14 Attendance Monitoring

Corrections, oversight, and what auto-adjusts versus what needs a manual hand.

Viewing records

Go to Attendance → All Attendance, filter by employee, status, or date range. Late entries and auto-checkouts are visually flagged.

Manual corrections

Editing a check-in or check-out time directly on a record is sometimes necessary — device downtime, a missed checkin, and so on.

Corrections don't cascade automatically If you manually change a check-in time in a way that would have avoided a late-arrival Permission deduction, that deduction is not automatically reversed. You'll need to refuse the associated auto-generated Permission leave record yourself if it's no longer warranted.

Refusing an AM half-day leave after a same-day check-in

If an employee has an approved AM half-day leave but ends up checking in that morning anyway (plans changed, emergency resolved, etc.), the system does not auto-correct anything — their leave stays approved and no late minutes are calculated, since the late threshold is still shifted to the afternoon start time for that day. Fixing this is a manual HR step:

  1. Go to Time Off → Management → Time Off and find the employee's AM leave record for that date.
  2. Open the record and click Refuse.
  3. Once refused, the system automatically recalculates that day's attendance against the normal 9:30 AM threshold — if the employee checked in late relative to 9:30 AM, it will now show as Late and draw from their Permission balance as usual.

No further action is needed after refusing — the recalculation happens immediately, you don't need to manually edit the attendance record itself.

Batch summary generation

Use Generate Summaries (Batch) to compute or recompute Monthly Summary records for all employees at once — useful at month-end before payroll, or after a bulk correction.

15 Company Holidays

Declared holidays, the automatic 2nd/4th Saturday rule, and how to turn a specific Saturday into a working day.

How to declare a holiday

  1. Go to Attendance → Company Holidays and click New.
  2. Enter the Holiday Name and Date.
  3. Select the Type: Public Holiday, Company Event, Emergency Closure, or Other.
  4. Leave Compensatory Working Day unchecked (this is only for turning a Saturday into a working day — see below).
  5. Save.

This immediately blocks check-in for that date, company-wide — employees attempting to check in will see a message explaining why.

Only one record per date You can't create two holiday records for the same date — the system blocks duplicates and tells you which existing record already covers that day.

2nd & 4th Saturday — fully automatic

These are treated as company-wide days off without you needing to declare anything. A background job keeps the Time Off calendar showing them as greyed-out/non-working for the current year and next year automatically.

Turning a specific Saturday into a working day

To make up for a mid-week closure (or any other reason), you can designate a specific Saturday as a working day instead:

  1. Go to Attendance → Company Holidays and click New.
  2. Set the Date to the Saturday you want to convert.
  3. Enable Compensatory Working Day.
  4. Save.
Only works on Saturdays If you try to enable Compensatory Working Day on any date that isn't a Saturday, the system blocks it with a validation error naming the actual day of the week.

Once saved, check-in works normally for employees on that date — even though it's a 2nd or 4th Saturday that would otherwise be a day off.

Editing or removing a holiday

Open the record from the Company Holidays list and edit or archive it as needed. Archiving (unchecking Active) removes it from the Time Off calendar and un-blocks check-in for that date, same as deleting would — but keeps the record for reference.

16 Employee Records

Uploading documents, confirming a new employee's ID, and assigning system permissions.

How to upload a document for an employee

  1. Open the employee's profile (Employees → [select employee]).
  2. Go to the Documents tab.
  3. Click New on the documents list.
  4. Enter a Document Name and select a Category: ID Proof, Educational Certificate, Contract/Agreement, Experience Letter, Medical Certificate, or Other.
  5. Attach the file and save.

Access levels differ HR officers have full access (upload, edit, delete). HR Managers can view documents but cannot edit or delete them from here. Employees do not have access to this tab at all — if an employee needs to submit a document, they hand it to HR to upload.

Confirming a new employee (Employee ID)

New employee records start in Draft — no Employee ID is assigned yet, and the record can be freely deleted if it was created by mistake.

  1. Open the new employee's profile.
  2. Verify all details are correct — especially the Department, since the Employee ID is generated from the department code and can't be changed after confirmation.
  3. Click Confirm Employee.

This generates a permanent Employee ID (format: year + department code + sequence number, e.g. 25CFD003) and locks the record — the Employee ID field becomes read-only, and the Department can no longer be changed on this record.

Confirmed employees can't be deleted Once confirmed, an employee record can only be archived, not deleted — this protects data integrity. If you need a genuine department transfer, archive the old record and create a new one rather than trying to edit the department.

Resetting an employee to Draft

Only a Manager (HR Manager group) can do this, and only if the employee has no payslips, attendance records, or leave allocations yet — the system blocks the reset otherwise and tells you which type of record is blocking it. If you reset and later re-confirm the same employee without changing their department, the same Employee ID is restored rather than consuming a new sequence number.

Assigning System Role & permissions

Every employee has a System Role field (Employee / HR / Manager) that determines what they can access in the system — separate from their job title.

  1. Open the employee's profile.
  2. Under Settings → System Access, set the System Role.
  3. Click Setup Permissions in the header.
Changing the role field alone does nothing Setting a new System Role does not, by itself, change what the employee can access. You must click Setup Permissions afterward for the change to actually apply — this is a deliberate manual step, not an oversight.

Employees in a department whose name contains "Manufacturing" automatically also receive manufacturing module access, regardless of their System Role — this is applied at the same time you click Setup Permissions.

17 CRM: Leads, Opportunities & Converting to a Project

Standard CRM usage for tracking client queries, plus the custom Convert to Project step once a deal is won.

Creating a lead or opportunity

  1. Go to CRM → My Pipeline (or CRM → Leads if lead scoring is enabled for your setup).
  2. Click New.
  3. Fill in the client/contact details, an Opportunity title, expected revenue, and assign a Salesperson and Sales Team if relevant.
  4. Save.

Moving a deal through the pipeline

CRM opportunities move through pipeline stages (e.g. New, Qualified, Proposal, Won) shown as kanban columns under CRM → My Pipeline. Drag a card to a new column to change its stage, or open the opportunity and use the stage bar at the top of the form.

  • Use the Log a Note / Activities tools on the opportunity to track calls, meetings, and follow-ups as you work the deal.
  • Update Expected Revenue and Probability as the deal firms up — this feeds the pipeline forecast views under CRM → Reporting.

Converting a Won opportunity into a project

Once an opportunity is genuinely closed and won, and the work is ready to be tracked as a project, HR converts it directly from the CRM record — there's no need to manually recreate the project and re-enter the client details.

  1. Open the opportunity (CRM → [select the opportunity]).
  2. Make sure its stage is set to Won — the Convert to Project button won't work otherwise.
  3. Click Convert to Project in the header.

Finding the linked project

Who can convert

The Convert to Project button is only visible to HR officers and Managers — regular salespeople and employees don't see it, even on their own Won opportunities.

The new project starts unassigned to a team Converting doesn't automatically assign the new project to a Team — you (or a Manager) need to open the project afterward and set its Team, the same way you would for any manually created project, before it shows up properly in employees' team-scoped project views (see Section 06B).

18 Sales, Purchase & Inventory

These three run as standard, unmodified Odoo 19 Community apps. This section summarizes official Odoo documentation for the day-to-day flows you'll actually use.

Sales — quotation to order

The sales flow moves through four stages: Quotation (a proposal sent to the customer) → Sales Order (created automatically once the customer accepts) → Delivery (products shipped, if applicable) → Invoice (the final bill).

  1. Go to Sales → Orders → Quotations and click New.
  2. Select the Customer — this is mandatory. Once selected, the Invoice Address and Delivery Address auto-fill from the customer's saved details.
  3. Under the Order Lines tab, click Add a product and add each item or service, with quantity and unit price.
  4. Fill in any other relevant details — payment terms, pricelist, expiration date.
  5. Click Send by Email to send the quotation to the customer, or Print for a PDF copy.
  6. Once the customer accepts, click Confirm — this turns the quotation into a confirmed Sales Order and assigns it an official order number.
Confirming a Sales Order triggers Inventory automatically If the Inventory app is installed (it is, in this system) and the product isn't a Service, confirming a Sales Order automatically creates a Delivery in Inventory. You can jump straight to it using the Delivery smart button at the top of the confirmed order.

Purchase — Request for Quotation to Purchase Order

An RFQ (Request for Quotation) is your internal draft document — it isn't seen by the vendor until you send it.

  1. Go to Purchase → Orders → Requests for Quotation and click New.
  2. Select the Vendor.
  3. Under the Products tab, click Add a product, and add each item with quantity. If the product has a saved vendor price on its product form, this auto-populates.
  4. Click Send by Email to send it to the vendor as a PDF, or Print RFQ for a hard copy.
  5. Once the vendor agrees to the price and terms, click Confirm Order. This converts the RFQ into a confirmed Purchase Order (PO).
Confirming a PO triggers a pending Receipt in Inventory Once confirmed, Odoo automatically creates a pending goods receipt in Inventory (Inventory → Operations → Receipts), shown as Waiting until the vendor actually delivers the goods.

Comparing quotes from multiple vendors

If you're sourcing the same products from more than one vendor, you can send separate RFQs to each and compare their responses side-by-side (price, delivery time, terms) before confirming the one you want. This requires Purchase Agreements to be enabled under Purchase → Configuration → Settings.

Inventory — receiving and shipping

By default, this system uses one-step receipts and deliveries — the simplest flow, where goods move directly in or out of stock in a single transfer.

Receiving goods (from a confirmed Purchase Order)

  1. Go to Inventory → Overview and open the Receipts card, or go to Inventory → Operations → Receipts.
  2. Find the receipt linked to your Purchase Order — it shows as Waiting until the goods physically arrive.
  3. Once the goods arrive and are checked, open the receipt and click Validate.

Validating moves the products into warehouse stock and updates on-hand quantities immediately.

Shipping goods (from a confirmed Sales Order)

  1. Go to Inventory → Operations → Deliveries, or click the Delivery smart button on the confirmed sales order.
  2. Once the products have been picked, packed, and are ready to ship, click Validate on the delivery order.

Validating moves the products out of stock and marks the delivery as Done.

Multi-step warehouses work differently If a warehouse is ever reconfigured for 2-step or 3-step receipts/deliveries (e.g. to add a Quality Control or Packing stage), you'll see multiple transfers per order instead of one — each needs to be validated in sequence before the goods count as received or shipped. Check Inventory → Configuration → Warehouses if you're unsure which mode a warehouse is set to.

Checking stock levels

Go to Inventory → Reporting or open a product and check its On Hand quantity smart button to see current stock across all locations.

Standard Odoo documentation applies Since these three apps are unmodified, Odoo's own official documentation covers every feature here in full depth — this section is an orientation, not a replacement for it. Search "Odoo 19 Sales/Purchase/Inventory documentation" for anything not covered here.

20 Contacts — Auto Company ID

One custom addition to standard Contacts: every company contact gets a unique, auto-generated ID.

How it works

When you save a new contact marked as a Company (not an individual), the system automatically generates a unique Company ID the first time you click Save — visible as a read-only badge next to the contact's name.

  • Indian companies (Country = India) get an ID prefixed with N (e.g. N001SMSTECHGARMENTS).
  • Companies from any other country get an ID prefixed with IN (e.g. IN001ACMECORP).

The ID is built from a running sequence number plus the company name with spaces and special characters stripped out.

Country is mandatory for companies You can't save a contact marked as a Company without selecting a Country — the system blocks the save until it's set, since the Company ID's prefix depends on it.

If a contact is missing its Company ID

This can happen for contacts converted from an individual to a company, or ones created before this feature existed.

  1. Open the contact.
  2. Click Generate Company ID next to the name field — this button only appears if the contact is a Company and doesn't already have an ID.

Individual contacts under a company

A person linked to a company (via the Related Company field) shows that company's ID on their own contact form too, for quick reference — this is read-only and just mirrors the parent company's ID.

Where else this shows up

On a CRM lead or opportunity, the linked contact's Company ID appears as a badge next to the customer field, so you can identify the account at a glance while working the deal.

22 FAQ

Quick answers, filtered to your register.

What happens if I forget to check out?
The system auto-closes your session at 7:00 PM and flags it as an Auto Checkout on your record. HR and your Manager are notified automatically.
Can I request leave for a past date?
Not directly — only HR can backdate a leave request on your behalf.
What's the difference between Permission leave and regular leave?
Permission is a monthly 4-hour buffer specifically for late arrivals or short personal errands, requested in hours. Regular leave (CL/EL/ML) is requested in full or half days.
I have an approved Permission request covering my late arrival — will I get deducted twice?
No. If your manual Permission request already covers your actual check-in time, no additional minutes are deducted. The system only deducts extra minutes if you arrive later than what you requested.
I have an approved Morning half-day leave but checked in anyway — will I be marked late?
No, not automatically. Your leave stays approved and no late minutes are calculated. If this needs correcting, ask HR to manually refuse the leave — only then does lateness get recalculated.
Can I edit a task I didn't create?
Only if you're a Manager, or HR. Otherwise, you can still progress its state (Start, Mark Complete, Verify, Close) and log timesheet entries if it's assigned to you, but other fields stay read-only.
Can I see other employees' timesheet entries?
Yes — every employee can view all timesheet entries company-wide. You can only create, edit, or delete your own.
I assigned a task to someone on a different team — why didn't it assign right away?
Cross-team assignments need approval first. The system creates an assignment request and notifies Managers/HR; the task shows as pending for that person until it's decided. Managers can assign across teams without this step.
Can I view a Case Study I'm not assigned to?
Yes — every case study is viewable by any logged-in user. You just can't edit it unless you're in its Assigned Employees list, or you're HR.
Can I approve one half of a half-day leave and reject the other?
Yes — Morning and Afternoon halves are separate records, so they can be approved or refused independently.
An employee had an approved Morning leave but checked in anyway — why doesn't the system fix this automatically?
By design, it doesn't. The leave stays approved and no late minutes are calculated until you manually refuse the leave record from Time Off → Approvals. Once refused, lateness recalculates automatically against the normal 9:30 AM threshold.
Who can approve a project completion request?
Only Managers — HR can submit a completion request on a team's behalf, but cannot approve it. This is one of the few approvals in the system where HR is deliberately excluded.
Who can convert a CRM opportunity into a project?
Only HR officers and Manager see the Convert to Project button, and only once the opportunity's stage is Won. It's a one-time, irreversible action.
I changed an employee's System Role — why don't their permissions reflect it yet?
Changing the role field alone does nothing. You have to click Setup Permissions on their profile afterward for the new access to actually apply.
How do I tell an auto-deducted Permission leave from a manually requested one?
Auto-generated records from late-arrival deductions are flagged internally and don't trigger the standard "your leave was approved" notification, to avoid confusing the employee.
What if Permission overflow doesn't show up on a payslip?
Confirm the Monthly Summary for that employee and month before recomputing the payslip — overflow minutes flow through the confirmed summary, not directly from attendance.
My role looks wrong / I can't see a section I should have access to.
Click "Refresh My Access" in the header — it re-checks your role from the ERP. If it's still wrong, your System Role needs updating; contact HR.
