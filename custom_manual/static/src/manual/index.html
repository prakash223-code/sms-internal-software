Manual / Overview

00 Overview

The SMS Enterprises ERP runs on ODOO 19, extended with a custom addon suite covering attendance, leave, payroll, projects, expenses, and documents.

What the system covers

  • Attendance — check-in/check-out, late detection, half-day leave logic, company holidays.
  • Leave — Casual, Earned, Medical, and Permission leave, with anniversary-based allocation and carry-forward.
  • Payroll — monthly salary computation, driven by your attendance and leave register.
  • Projects — team-based tasks, kanban boards, cross-team assignment approval.
  • Expenses — submission, approval, and Excel export for records.
  • Documents — employee document upload with inline preview.
How access works here The sections you see are read directly from your employee role in the ERP — the same role that already controls your permissions elsewhere in the system. There's nothing to select; it's not something this page can get wrong or something you can override from the browser.
Manual / Your Desk

01 Attendance & Check-In

One check-in, one check-out, once a day. The system tracks lateness automatically.

Checking in and out

Open Check In / Check Out from the Attendances menu. The same button toggles — if you're already checked in, it checks you out. You can only complete one full cycle per day.

Late threshold — 9:30 AM Checking in after 9:30 AM marks the day as Late and starts eating into your monthly Permission buffer (see Section 03). The threshold shifts to the afternoon start time on days you have an approved AM half-day leave.

If you don't check out

The system auto-closes any open session at 7:00 PM your local time. This is flagged as an Auto Checkout on your record — worth a habit check if it happens often.

Holidays block check-in

On declared company holidays and the 2nd/4th Saturday of the month, check-in is disabled with a message explaining why. Check-out still works if you were already checked in (e.g. a compensatory working day changes this — see Section 13).

02 Leave & Half-Days

Three leave types, an anniversary-based year, and half-days split cleanly into AM and PM.

Leave types & entitlement

TypeAnnual QuotaCarry-Forward
Casual Leave (CL)12 daysUp to 50% (max 6 days)
Earned Leave (EL)3 daysFull carry-forward
Medical Leave (ML)3 daysFull carry-forward

Your leave year runs from your joining anniversary, not the calendar year. On your anniversary, unused leave carries forward automatically per the table above, and a fresh quota opens.

Requesting leave

  1. Go to Time Off → New
  2. Choose the leave type and dates
  3. For a single day, choose Full Day or Half Day (AM/PM)
  4. Submit — it routes to HR for approval

Half-day mechanics

A half-day leave is recorded as its own leave entry for that morning or afternoon. You can mix types on the same day — for example, Casual Leave in the morning and Earned Leave in the afternoon.

If you check in during your AM leave Checking in before the afternoon session start (typically 2:00 PM) on a day you have an approved AM half-day leave is treated as a change of plan — HR is notified and the leave is reviewed. Checking in at or after the afternoon start is treated as you legitimately working your PM half; the AM leave stands.
No backdating Leave can't be requested for past dates unless HR enters it on your behalf. Plan ahead where you can.

03 Permission Buffer

A monthly 4-hour cushion for late arrivals — used automatically, or claimed proactively.

How it works

Every employee gets a flat 4 hours (240 minutes) of Permission leave each month. It doesn't carry forward, and there's no proration for mid-month joiners — everyone starts the month fresh.

Manual requests

You can also draw on the same pool proactively — for a dentist appointment, say — by submitting a Permission leave request in hours, same as any other leave type.

If the pool runs out Once your 4-hour buffer is exhausted for the month, further late minutes are deducted directly from salary in that month's payroll, under the overflow deduction rule.

You'll get a notification when your balance drops to 30 minutes or below, and again if it hits zero.

10 Leave Policy & Allocations

How the anniversary-based leave year, quotas, and carry-forward are administered.

Policy at a glance

TypeQuotaCarry-Forward Cap
Casual Leave12 days / year6 days (50%)
Earned Leave3 days / year3 days (full)
Medical Leave3 days / year3 days (full)

Initial allocation

New employees receive their first cycle's allocation automatically on record creation, based on their joining date. No manual action needed unless the automated run is interrupted.

Anniversary carry-forward

A scheduled job runs daily and processes any employee whose joining-date anniversary falls that day — closing the previous leave year and opening a new one with carry-forward applied per the caps above.

Manual allocation edits If you need to adjust an employee's allocation directly, go to Time Off → Allocations, edit the amount, and approve the record for it to take effect.

Permission leave allocation

A separate monthly job grants every active employee a flat 4-hour Permission allocation on the 1st of each month — no carry-forward, no proration.

11 Payroll Processing

Monthly payroll draws directly from the attendance and leave register — run summaries before payslips.

Order matters Always run Attendance → Monthly Summary before generating payroll for the month. This is what feeds Permission overflow minutes into the salary deduction rule — skip it and the deduction won't appear on the payslip.

What feeds the payslip

  • Unpaid absent days — absences plus unpaid leave, from the monthly summary
  • Permission overflow — late minutes beyond the 4-hour monthly buffer, deducted at the per-minute rate
  • Base salary & allowances — from the employee's contract

12 Attendance Monitoring

Corrections, oversight, and what auto-adjusts versus what needs a manual hand.

Viewing records

Go to Attendance → All Attendance, filter by employee, status, or date range. Late entries and auto-checkouts are visually flagged.

Manual corrections

Editing a check-in or check-out time directly on a record is sometimes necessary — device downtime, a missed punch, and so on.

Corrections don't cascade automatically If you manually change a check-in time in a way that would have avoided a late-arrival Permission deduction, that deduction is not automatically reversed. You'll need to refuse the associated auto-generated Permission leave record yourself if it's no longer warranted.

Batch summary generation

Use Generate Summaries (Batch) to compute or recompute Monthly Summary records for all employees at once — useful at month-end before payroll, or after a bulk correction.

13 Company Holidays

Declared holidays, the 2nd/4th Saturday rule, and compensatory working days.

Declaring a holiday

Go to Attendance → Company Holidays → New. Set the date and type (Public Holiday, Company Event, Emergency Closure). This immediately blocks check-in for that date.

2nd & 4th Saturday

These are treated as company-wide days off automatically — no need to declare them individually.

Compensatory working Saturdays

To turn a specific Saturday into a working day (to make up for a mid-week closure, for example), create a holiday record for that Saturday and enable Compensatory Working Day. This overrides the default off-day rule for that date only, and only works on Saturdays.

15 FAQ

Quick answers, filtered to your register.

What happens if I forget to check out?
The system auto-closes your session at 7:00 PM and flags it as an Auto Checkout on your record.
Can I request leave for a past date?
Not directly — only HR can backdate a leave request on your behalf.
What's the difference between Permission leave and regular leave?
Permission is a monthly 4-hour buffer specifically for late arrivals or short personal errands, billed in hours. Regular leave (CL/EL/ML) is billed in full or half days.
Can I approve one half of a half-day leave and reject the other?
Yes — AM and PM halves are separate records, so they can be approved or refused independently.
How do I tell an auto-deducted Permission leave from a manually requested one?
Auto-generated records from late-arrival deductions are flagged internally and don't trigger the standard "your leave was approved" notification, to avoid confusing the employee.
What if Permission overflow doesn't show up on a payslip?
Run the Monthly Summary for that employee and month before recomputing payroll — overflow minutes flow through the summary, not directly from attendance.
My role looks wrong / I can't see a section I should have access to.
Click "Refresh My Access" in the header — it re-checks your role from the ERP. If it's still wrong, your employee_role field in HR needs updating; contact HR.
